Frequently Asked Questions

Questions About Selling Your House for Cash

Most homeowners ask the same questions before their first call. Here are straight answers — no fine print.

How do you calculate your cash offer?
We base our offers on three things: the after-repair value of similar homes recently sold in your neighborhood, the cost of repairs and updates the home will need, and a fair margin for our team. We're transparent about all three numbers — when we present the offer, we walk you through exactly how we got there.
Are there any fees, commissions, or closing costs?
No. The number on our offer is the number you net at the closing table. We pay all standard closing costs. No agent commissions. No service fees. No surprise deductions.
How fast can you close?
As fast as 7 days from accepting our offer, depending on the title work. If you need more time — say, 30, 60, or even 90 days — we'll close on the date that works for you.
Do I need to clean or repair the house first?
Not at all. Leave behind anything you don't want, take anything you do, and we'll handle the rest. Roof needs replacing, foundation cracked, fire damage, hoarding situation — we've seen it. We buy as-is.
Are you really paying cash?
Yes. We close with funds in escrow — no buyer financing, no mortgage contingency, no risk of falling through. Our offers don't depend on a bank.
What if I'm behind on my mortgage or facing foreclosure?
That's one of the situations we specialize in. We can move quickly — often before the foreclosure process completes — and structure the deal so you walk away with cash and your credit intact. Reach out as early as possible; the more time we have, the more options we have.
Will I get a fair offer, or a lowball?
We offer what we'd genuinely pay — full stop. Lowball offers waste everyone's time, including ours. If our offer doesn't meet your needs, you walk away with no obligation. We'd rather lose a deal than damage our reputation.
What types of homes do you buy?
Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, multi-family properties (2–4 units), mobile homes, and land. We focus on residential properties in the $250K–$1M range across the area.
Is there any obligation to accept the offer?
Zero. The offer is yours to take, leave, or use to compare against other options. No pressure tactics. No follow-up sales calls. If we're not the right fit, we move on.
How is Silver Lynx different from a national iBuyer?
National iBuyers are algorithm-driven and typically charge service fees of 5–14%. They also tend to renegotiate after inspection. We're a local team — real people making real decisions — with no service fees and offers that don't change after we shake hands.
